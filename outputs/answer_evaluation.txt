**Improved Answer:**

"Overfitting occurs when a machine learning model learns the training data, including its noise and specific nuances, too precisely, rather than identifying the underlying general patterns. As a result, while the model performs exceptionally well on the data it was trained on (often exhibiting very low training error), it fails to generalize effectively to new, previously unseen data, leading to significantly worse performance on that validation or test set. This typically happens when the model is overly complex for the amount or nature of the training data, essentially memorizing the training examples rather than truly understanding the problem it's trying to solve."
